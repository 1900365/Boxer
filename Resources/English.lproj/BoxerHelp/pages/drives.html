Adding and removing DOS drives

Go to Emulation ▸ Show DOS Drives to open the Drives Inspector. This panel shows what DOS drives are available and lets you add, remove, and swap between drives.

To add a drive to DOS:

  • If you have a physical CD, insert it into your Mac’s DVD drive. Boxer will add it automatically to DOS.

    Boxer will do likewise when you insert a 3.5" floppy disk into a USB floppy drive connected to your Mac.

  • If you have a folder or disc image, drag it into the game window or the Drives Inspector. Boxer will add it as a new drive of the appropriate type (hard disk for regular folders, CD-ROM or floppy disk for disc images).

  • If you need more control over the drive type and letter, click the button on the Drives Inspector: this will bring up a file picker with advanced drive options.

To swap between CDs/floppies:

When you add extra CDs or floppies, they will go into a queue with any previous CD/floppy, using the same drive letter. This ensures the game will always find its discs at the right place.

You can then swap between each CD/floppy in a queue:

  • Go to Emulation ▸ Next Disc to cycle through all the CDs/floppies in the queue; or
  • Go to the Drives Inspector and double-click a specific CD/floppy to swap to it.

If you want to have CDs/floppies on separate drive letters — e.g. one floppy at drive A and another at drive B — then add the drives manually with the button as described above.

To remove a drive from DOS:

  • Drag the drive out of the Drives Inspector until the cursor changes to a puff of smoke, and then release it. The drive will be removed from DOS and will disappear from the list.

  • If you just want to temporarily disable a drive, then select the drive and click the button: this removes the drive from DOS but leaves it in the drive list. To re-enable the drive, double-click the drive or click the button.

Bundling additional drives into a gamebox

Each of your gameboxes contains a drive C for the hard disk where the game is installed, and often a drive D for the game’s CD-ROM. Because they’re part of the gamebox, these drives are always available in DOS when you run the game.

If you want additional drives to be always available to the game — for instance, extra CDs for a multi-CD game — then you can bundle them into the gamebox too:

  1. Add the drive to DOS if it isn’t already, by following the instructions above.
  2. In the Drives Inspector, select the drive and click on the icon; or, right-click on the drive and choose Import into Gamebox.
  3. Make a cup of tea while the drive imports.

Once the import is finished, the drive will be a permanent part of the gamebox and always there when you run the game.

The import process will physically copy the drive’s contents into the gamebox, creating a duplicate of the original folder or image used for the drive. You don’t need to keep the original around after this.
